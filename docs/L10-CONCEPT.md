# The Level 10 Meeting (L10)

L10 Manager is built around the **Level 10 Meeting**, the weekly leadership-team meeting format from the EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System) / *Traction* methodology. If you're new to EOS, this page is the vocabulary reference the rest of the project (docs, code, UI copy) sticks to.

## The standard agenda

A traditional L10 runs ~90 minutes, same day/time every week, with a strict agenda:

| # | Segment | What happens |
|---|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Segue** | Each person shares one piece of good news (personal or business). Sets tone, takes ~5 min. |
| 2 | **Scorecard** | Review the team's weekly measurables (a handful of numbers per person) — each one is simply on-track or off-track. |
| 3 | **Rock Review** | Check status of this quarter's Rocks (the 90-day priorities) — on-track or off-track, no discussion yet. |
| 4 | **Customer/Employee Headlines** | Quick-fire good/bad news about customers or employees — headlines only, no solving. |
| 5 | **To-Do List** | Review last week's to-dos — done or not done. |
| 6 | **IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve)** | The heart of the meeting (~60 min). Anything off-track above becomes an *issue* dropped onto the Issues List; the team identifies the real problem, discusses it, and solves it — usually producing a new to-do, a rock, or a decision. |
| 7 | **Conclude** | Recap the new to-dos, decide what (if anything) needs to be cascaded to the rest of the org, then everyone rates the meeting 1–10. |

## How this maps to L10 Manager's three phases

L10 Manager organizes work around **Prep → Run → Review**, which lines up with the agenda like this:

- **Prep** (before the meeting): update the Scorecard numbers, gather Headlines, check Rock status, and make sure the Issues List and To-Do List are current — so the meeting itself doesn't burn time collecting information.
- **Run** (during the meeting): step through Segue → Scorecard → Rocks → Headlines → To-Do List → IDS → Conclude in real time, keeping the meeting on the rails and on the clock.
- **Review** (after the meeting): send the cascading message, make sure new to-dos and issues are captured, and track the meeting rating over time.

## Terminology to reuse everywhere

Stick to the terms above (Segue, Scorecard, Rock, Headline, To-Do, Issue, IDS, Conclude, cascading message) rather than inventing synonyms — anyone who already runs L10s will recognize them immediately, and it keeps the project's docs/UI/code consistent.
